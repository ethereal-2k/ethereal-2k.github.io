About Me

I’m a systems-focused engineer (Rutgers CS, May 2025) building resilient data and compute pipelines end-to-end—from ingestion and transport to storage, orchestration, and observability. I work across distributed systems, high-performance networking, parallel compute, and ML/analytics on both cloud and bare metal. I prioritize predictable latency, cache-friendly data paths, circuit-breakers, and failure containment with clear SLIs/SLOs. Recent projects include GPU-accelerated packet authentication, CPU-optimized IP lookup, and blockchain-backed auditability for multi-party exchanges. I enjoy making complex systems observable, cost-aware, and production-ready.

Secure Blockchain Integration Approach for Knowledge Discovery in Industrial Internet of Things

IEEE Xplore, 2025

The paper proposes a four-layer security architecture that isolates IIoT devices from user-facing layers and maintains a registry to block malicious endpoints. By combining proxy re-encryption for clients, PoA consensus for blockchain nodes, and whitelist-based access control for IIoT devices, it enforces legitimacy across participants. A private Ethereum PoA prototype measures end-to-end exchange time and gas consumption while supporting accountable knowledge discovery.
